# ProcessHarbor External Validation Kit

**Purpose:** Produce genuine, ethical external evidence without changing the application or triggering a deployment.

## Study objective

Evaluate whether first-time users can transform fragmented operational notes into a useful, reviewable documentation package using the current ProcessHarbor workflow.

## Research questions

1. Can participants understand what the product is for within 60 seconds?
2. Can they load or enter operational notes and generate structured output?
3. Can they distinguish SOP, checklist, knowledge-base, and gap findings?
4. Can they find and understand versions, audit activity, and export?
5. Which labels, screens, or transitions cause hesitation?
6. Do participants trust the output appropriately, or do they overestimate its authority?
7. What would prevent them from using the tool in real operational work?

## Recommended sample

- Five participants for the first round
- Mix of operations, retail leadership, administrative, documentation, training, support, or small-business experience
- Avoid recruiting only close collaborators who already understand the product
- Record prior familiarity with AI and process documentation

Five participants are not statistically representative. The study is intended to identify recurring usability problems and produce transparent formative evidence.

## Participant invitation

> I am evaluating a portfolio application that turns rough operational notes into structured SOPs, onboarding checklists, knowledge-base drafts, and documentation gap findings. The session takes approximately 25-35 minutes. I am testing the application, not you. Your honest confusion and criticism are valuable. Participation is voluntary, and no sensitive workplace information should be entered.

## Consent script

Before starting, confirm:

- participation is voluntary;
- the participant may stop at any time;
- no confidential employer, customer, medical, legal, financial, or personally identifying information should be entered;
- screen or audio recording requires explicit permission;
- quotes will not be published without separate approval;
- results may be reported in aggregated or de-identified form;
- the application is a portfolio product and not a production record system.

## Standard test scenario

Use the same fictional notes for each participant:

> New employees learn returns differently depending on who trains them. A supervisor should approve refunds above $100, but the approval method is not documented. Customer receipts should be checked, damaged items need photos, and suspected fraud should be escalated to asset protection. Training is considered complete after observation, but nobody records the observation date. Weekly policy updates are mentioned in meetings but are not stored in one place.

## Tasks

### Task 1: Orientation

Without coaching, ask the participant to explain:

- what they think the application does;
- who it appears to be for;
- what they expect to accomplish.

**Success signal:** A substantially correct explanation within 60 seconds.

### Task 2: Generate documentation

Ask the participant to use the fictional notes to create structured documentation.

**Observe:**

- whether they find the input area;
- whether they understand the generation action;
- whether they wait for or recognize completion;
- what output they inspect first.

### Task 3: Evaluate the SOP

Ask:

- What is usable?
- What is missing?
- What would need human confirmation?
- Is ownership and escalation clear?

### Task 4: Review supporting outputs

Ask the participant to locate:

- onboarding or training checklist;
- knowledge-base content;
- documentation gaps;
- versions or audit activity.

Do not tell them where these controls are unless they become blocked.

### Task 5: Export

Ask the participant to export the workspace and explain what they believe the export contains.

### Task 6: Trust and boundaries

Ask:

- Would you treat this output as a final policy?
- What review would be needed before workplace use?
- What information would you avoid entering?
- Which feature appears automated but is not sufficiently explained?

## Severity rubric

- **Critical:** Data loss, severe privacy misunderstanding, destructive action, or inability to complete the core workflow.
- **High:** Most participants cannot complete a primary task or materially misunderstand output authority.
- **Medium:** Meaningful friction, repeated hesitation, unclear terminology, or a recoverable workflow failure.
- **Low:** Minor polish, wording, alignment, or discoverability issue.
- **Informational:** Preference or future opportunity without demonstrated task harm.

### Recurring findings

For every finding, include:

- evidence;
- number of affected participants;
- severity;
- likely cause;
- proposed change;
- whether corrected;
- retest result.

### Claim-safe conclusion

Use language such as:

> Five participants completed a structured formative usability study. The results identify usability patterns in this sample; they do not establish market demand, production suitability, or population-wide usability.

Do not use:

- “users love it”;
- “market validated”;
- “proven intuitive”;
- “100% successful” without context;
- selectively positive testimonials that conceal major failures.

## Retest protocol

After corrections:

1. Use two or three participants who did not join round one.
2. Reuse the same core tasks.
3. Compare assistance rate, task time, and repeated findings.
4. Report regressions and unresolved findings.
5. Preserve the original results rather than replacing them.

## Completion evidence

The validation package is complete only when it contains:

- participant consent records;
- de-identified observation sheets;
- raw task outcomes;
- questionnaire responses;
- honest finding severity;
- correction log;
- retest results where available;
- separately approved quotations;
- a claim-safe public summary.
